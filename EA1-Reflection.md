# EA1 Reflection – Spokane CyberCup CTF

Note: Claude Sonnet was used to improve grammar and flow.

## Activity Description

The expectation for EA1 was to participate in a real-world cybersecurity event and reflect on the experience. I attended the Spokane CyberCup, a Capture the Flag (CTF) competition hosted in Spokane, with teammates Aaron Aytes and Taylor Tran. With just over 50 competing teams and a maximum team size of five, our three-person team was on the smaller side. The competition presented a large list of categorized challenges, each solvable by finding a hidden "flag" (secret key) to earn points. Categories ranged from SQL injection and AI prompt manipulation to OSINT, physical lock picking, and general cybersecurity knowledge. Our team finished 26th overall, a result I was proud of, given it was our first competition.

## Technical Decisions

My first technical decision was to start with OSINT challenges, which turned out to be one of the hardest categories in the competition. I committed significant time to it before ultimately solving it, but in hindsight this was a poor prioritization call. The better strategy, which I recognized too late, was to sweep through all easier problems first to maximize early points, then tackle harder ones. Within my areas of strength, I took the lead on SQL injection challenges and owned most of the coding-based problems, as I was the strongest programmer on the team.

## Contributions

This was a team effort between myself, Aaron Aytes, and Taylor Tran. My individual contributions included solving the OSINT challenge independently, handling the majority of SQL injection problems, and taking on most coding-oriented challenges. I served as the team's primary resource for programming tasks throughout the competition.

## Quality Assessment

Overall, I would rate my participation positively. I contributed meaningful points and grew a lot in a short time. That said, my biggest mistake was poor time management early on, spending too long on a single hard problem instead of building momentum with easier wins first. If I could redo this competition, I would start with a broad pass through all categories to knock out low-hanging fruit. I would also come in with more well-rounded preparation across all topic areas rather than depth in just one or two. Breadth matters more in CTF competitions given how many different challenge types appear.
